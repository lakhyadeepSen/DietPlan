Balanced Pure Vegetarian Diet Plan (No Onion, No Garlic)

Breakfast Options:

  • Oatmeal with milk, topped with banana and a spoonful of peanut butter
  • Paneer bhurji (without onion-garlic) with whole wheat roti and a small bowl of yogurt
  • Moong dal cheela (without onion-garlic) with vegetable stuffing and a glass of buttermilk
  • Besan chilla (without onion-garlic) with tomato-cucumber salad and a small cup of curd

Lunch Options:

  • Rice, dal (with cumin, asafoetida tempering), seasonal vegetable sabzi, and raita
  • Whole wheat roti, rajma/chole (made with ginger, cumin, and asafoetida), cucumber-tomato salad, and a small bowl of curd
  • Vegetable pulao (without onion-garlic, using cumin and other spices) with plain yogurt and roasted papad
  • Lentil khichdi with vegetable curry (with ginger, hing, and spices) and cucumber raita

Dinner Options:

  • Roti with mixed vegetable curry (using asafoetida, cumin, ginger for flavor) and a small bowl of dal
  • Palak paneer (without onion-garlic) with roti and cucumber salad
  • Masoor dal with cumin tempering, roti, seasonal vegetable, and a small serving of rice
  • Soya chunk curry (with tomatoes, ginger, and spices) with roti and cucumber-tomato salad

Snack Options:

  • Handful of mixed nuts (peanuts, almonds)
  • Roasted chickpeas
  • Sprouts salad with lemon dressing
  • Fruit (apple, orange, guava)
  • Buttermilk or lassi
  • Paneer cubes with black pepper
  • Ragi or jowar porridge
  • Sattu drink (roasted gram flour mixed with water and spices)

Key Nutrition Components

Protein Sources (crucial for strength building):

  • Dairy: Paneer, curd/yogurt, milk, cheese (in moderation)
  • Legumes: Various dals, chickpeas, rajma, soybean products
  • Nuts and seeds: Peanuts, almonds, flaxseeds, chia seeds

Complex Carbs (for energy):

  • Whole grains: Brown rice, whole wheat, oats, millets like ragi and jowar
  • Starchy vegetables: Sweet potatoes, potatoes, peas

Healthy Fats:

  • Ghee (in moderation)
  • Coconut oil for cooking
  • Seeds and nuts
  • Avocado (if available and affordable)

Vegetables (for micronutrients and fiber):

  • Green leafy vegetables: Spinach, fenugreek, amaranth
  • Other vegetables: Cauliflower, cabbage, carrots, bell peppers, tomatoes, cucumber

Budget-Friendly Tips

  1. Focus on seasonal vegetables - they're cheaper and more nutritious
  2. Buy legumes and grains in bulk - they're inexpensive sources of protein and carbs
  3. Replace expensive nuts with peanuts - they provide similar benefits at a lower cost
  4. Prepare homemade yogurt/curd - cheaper than store-bought and can be used in multiple ways
  5. Grow sprouts at home - extremely economical source of nutrition
  6. Use local millets like ragi, jowar instead of quinoa or imported grains
  7. Frozen vegetables are affordable options when fresh ones are expensive
  8. Make vegetable stock at home using vegetable scraps for flavor without onion-garlic
  9. Grow herbs like coriander and curry leaves in small pots at home

Meal Timing for Your Goals

  • Eat protein with every meal to support muscle recovery and growth
  • Heavier meals earlier in the day - larger breakfast and lunch, lighter dinner
  • Pre-workout snack (fruit or small amount of nuts) about 30-45 minutes before exercise
  • Post-workout nutrition within 30-60 minutes (protein-rich meal or snack)
  • Stay hydrated - aim for 2-3 liters of water daily
  • Evening milk - continue your habit of milk in the evening for calcium and protein

Portion Control Tips

For weight loss, focus on portion control rather than eliminating food groups:

  • Use smaller plates
  • Fill half your plate with vegetables, one-quarter with protein, one-quarter with complex carbs
  • Be mindful of added fats in cooking
  • Listen to hunger cues rather than eating by the clock

Things to Avoid

  • Meat, eggs, and fish (already being avoided)
  • Onion and garlic in any form
  • Packaged foods with hidden onion/garlic powder or extracts (check ingredients)
  • Excessive fried foods or processed foods
  • High-sugar foods and beverages
  • Alcohol and excessive caffeinated beverages

Flavor Enhancers to Replace Onion and Garlic

  • Asafoetida (hing) - small pinch adds savory flavor
  • Cumin seeds (jeera) - for tempering dals and vegetables
  • Ginger - adds depth of flavor
  • Fresh coriander - for garnish and flavor
  • Lemon juice - adds tanginess
  • Black pepper - for spice without onion/garlic
  • Green chilies - if you tolerate spice
  • Curry leaves - adds aroma and flavor

Sample Daily Meal Plan

  • Early Morning: Warm water with lemon
  • Breakfast: Besan chilla (without onion-garlic) with tomato-cucumber salad and curd
  • Mid-Morning: Fruit (apple or guava) or a handful of mixed nuts
  • Lunch: Rice, masoor dal with cumin tempering, seasonal vegetable sabzi, cucumber raita
  • Evening: Glass of milk (as per your routine) with a small snack like roasted chickpeas
  • Dinner: 2 rotis with palak paneer (without onion-garlic) and simple cucumber salad
